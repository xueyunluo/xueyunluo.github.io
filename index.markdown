I am a PhD candidate at Cornell SC Johnson College of Business, advised by <a href="https://business.cornell.edu/faculty-research/faculty/cmf257/" style="color: inherit; text-decoration: none;"><u>Professor Chris Forman</u></a>.

        <p>My research interest sits at the intersection of economics of digitization, information systems, and business strategy. My research focuses on two main streams: 
        (1) <b>technology adoption and labor markets</b>, where I leverage U.S. Census data and large-scale online workforce data to examine how information technology reshapes firms’ human capital strategies and workers’ career trajectory; 
        and (2) <b>platform governance and supply-side reallocation</b>, where I study how changes in platform design and content on the supply side reallocate engagement, profits, and attention 
        among suppliers. </p>
        <p>Methodologically, my work involves econometrics and randomized control trials to understand the causal impacts of platform governance policy and digitization strategy. 
        I also adapt and integrate machine learning tools for social and economic empirical studies.</p>
